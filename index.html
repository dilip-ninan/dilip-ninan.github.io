About

I am a philosophy professor at Tufts University. Most of my research is in philosophy of language and formal semantics, but I am also interested in epistemology, philosophy of mind, metaphysics, and philosophical logic.

I co-direct the Linguistics Minor at Tufts and teach courses including Semantics, Philosophy of Language, and Modal Logic. I hold a BA from the University of Western Ontario, a BPhil from Oxford University, and a PhD from MIT.

From 2019 to 2024, I was Editor-in-Chief of Linguistics and Philosophy. During the 2020 to 2021 academic year, I was a fellow at the Paris Institute for Advanced Study. Two articles were selected for inclusion in the Philosopher's Annual (2018 and 2024).
